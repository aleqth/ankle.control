What this is

ankle.control rebuilds an image (the reference) out of tiled copies of another image (the asset) — default asset is the ankle silhouette. Two modes share the same source and same brush:

  • MOSAIC — 2D. Tile the reference with the asset, sized and rotated by per-tile brightness and contrast. Save PNG (hi-res) renders at the reference's native resolution.
  • TERRAIN — 3D. Each tile becomes an instanced billboard in space, positioned at its (x,y) in the picture, pushed forward by per-pixel luminance, tinted to the reference colour. Head-on it reads as the picture; drag to orbit and see the depth. Add motion (wave / pulse / twist), pick or upload a background.

Both modes use whatever you upload as the asset PNG — so the Mona Lisa can be reassembled out of ankles, hearts, your face, or any silhouette. Source: github.com/aleqth/ankle.control.
